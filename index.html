Introduction

Two Lives, One Connection

Historical Figure

Jim Whetsel

Jim Whetsel lived during one of the most turbulent and transformative decades in American history — the 1960s. A time of civil rights movements, the Space Race, the Vietnam War, and a cultural revolution that changed the nation forever. His story is a window into what everyday life looked like during this pivotal era.

Present Day

Jack Whetsel

Jack Whetsel is a student living in the modern era — a world shaped by the very events Jim lived through. By comparing their lives side by side, we can see how much has changed, and how much stays the same. History is not just dates and battles; it lives in the people who experience it.

Then & Now

The 1960s vs. The 2020s

The 1960s — Jim's World
vs
The 2020s — Jack's World
Technology

Black & white televisions, rotary phones, and transistor radios. NASA's space program was the most advanced technology most Americans had ever witnessed.

Technology

Smartphones, social media, AI, and streaming on demand. More computing power fits in a pocket than existed in the entire world in 1960.

Transportation

Jim bought a used scooter for $200 and a car for $1.00, riding 15–20 miles just to visit a Dairy Queen. Gas cost around 31¢ a gallon.

Transportation

Jack got a silver 2014 Ford F-150 from his grandpa at 16. Gas costs around $3–4 a gallon, and GPS navigation fits on a phone screen.

Work & Money

Jim sorted glass bottles for 2¢–5¢ each, picked wild strawberries, and baled hay — saving every cent for months to afford a $200 scooter.

Work & Money

Jack runs his own lawn mowing business — 3 to 5 yards at $30 each — depositing earnings straight into a savings account, building toward his future as an electrician.

School

Hayes High School, Delaware, Ohio. No internet, no calculators — homework meant books and handwriting. Jim graduated in 1963 into a country gripped by the Cold War.

School

Batavia High School, finishing junior year. Chromebooks, Google Classroom, and online resources — but also harder decisions, like quitting football for his health after sophomore year.

Sports & Fitness

Jim ran varsity track at Hayes High School, competing in relays across central Ohio. Physical labor — baling hay, sorting stock — kept him active every day.

Sports & Fitness

Jack played football until making the difficult choice to step away for health reasons. He now hits the gym every week — training on his own terms and his own schedule.

World Event They Remember

Jim was in school the day the Soviets launched Sputnik into orbit in 1957 — the shot heard around the world that started the Space Race and changed America forever.

World Event They Remember

Jack remembers the assassination of Charlie Kirk in 2025 — a shocking moment that stopped the country, much like Sputnik did for Jim's generation, proving that history keeps happening whether you're ready or not.

Comparative Timeline

A Life Side by Side

Jim Whetsel
Jack Whetsel
Late 1950s — Jim

Working at the Family Grocery Store

Jim begins working at his father's "Red and White" grocery store — the first self-serve store in Delaware, Ohio. His main job is sorting returned glass soda bottles (Crown, Pepsi, Coke) for 2¢ to 5¢ each. He saves every nickel and dime toward a motor scooter.

Early 2020s — Jack

Running His Own Lawn Mowing Business

Jack starts his own lawn mowing business, taking on 3–5 yards and charging $30 a lawn. Every dollar he earns goes straight into his savings account — the same hustle Jim had sorting bottles for nickels and dimes decades earlier.

Late 1950s — Jim

Odd Jobs & Sputnik Launches Into the Sky

In his spare time Jim picks wild strawberries along the railroad tracks and helps local farmers bale hay. At school one day, news breaks that the Soviet Union has launched Sputnik into orbit — a moment Jim never forgets, signaling the start of the Space Race.

2025 — Jack

Remembering the Assassination of Charlie Kirk

Just as Jim was at school when Sputnik launched and shook the country, Jack remembers the moment in 2025 when Charlie Kirk was assassinated — a jarring event that, like so many before it, reminded a generation that history happens whether you're ready for it or not.

Age 14 — Jim

The Red & White Cushman Pacemaker

At 14, Jim qualifies for a scooter license (restricted to under five brake horsepower). All those saved nickels pay off: he buys a used red and white Cushman Pacemaker for $200. With friends Kenny Wheeler and Rodney Goad, he rides 15–20 miles to Richwood, Ohio just to visit the Dairy Queen and pick up parts.

Age 16 — Jack

A Silver F-150 & His First License

At 16, Jack earns his driver's license — and his grandpa gifts him a silver 2014 Ford F-150 as his first car. He drives it around town, heads to the gym every week, and meets up with friends. Just like Jim's Chevy, the truck becomes a symbol of independence.

Age 15 — Jim

A 1950 Chevy for One Dollar

Uncle Earl can no longer drive due to health issues and sells Jim his 1950 Chevrolet for exactly $1.00. It is one of the most meaningful transactions of Jim's young life.

Mid 2020s — Jack

The Hardest Decision: Quitting Football

After his sophomore season, Jack makes one of the toughest calls of his life — walking away from football for health reasons. It takes courage to choose long-term wellbeing over a sport he loves, and the decision shapes who he becomes.

Early 1960s — Jim

Restoring the Chevy in Grandfather's Garage

Working in his grandfather's open garage, Jim restores the 1950 Chevy by hand — armed with only a drill, a sander attachment, and junkyard parts. The project teaches him patience, mechanical skill, and the satisfaction of building something with his own hands.

Mid 2020s — Jack

Building a Routine: Gym, Truck & Friends

With his F-150 and his license, Jack builds a rhythm — hitting the gym every week, driving around town, and spending time with his friends. Where Jim's independence was built on a scooter and junkyard parts, Jack's is built on early mornings and personal discipline.

Early 1960s — Jim

A Visit That Changes Everything

Uncertain about his future, a friend takes Jim to the Columbus College of Art and Design. Seeing the industrial design models on display, something clicks — he wants to design cars and products. That one visit sets the course for his entire career.

Present — Jack

A Path Forward: Electrician Internship

Just as Jim stood in that Columbus design school and saw his future, Jack is now working out how to land an electrician internship. A trade that is hands-on, skilled, and built to last — a different path than Jim's, but the same drive to build something real.

1963 — Jim

Graduation from Hayes High School

Jim graduates from Hayes High School in 1963 — the same year John F. Kennedy is assassinated and the country is gripped by upheaval. He steps into adulthood at one of the most electric and unsettled moments in American history.

2026 — Jack

Junior Year at Batavia High School

Jack is finishing his junior year at Batavia High School. The next chapter is close — just as Jim walked across the stage in 1963 and stepped into an uncertain world, Jack is approaching that same threshold, with his own version of the future waiting on the other side.
